HOUSTON CLAIM LEAVES ONE $1 MILLION PRIZE IN

TEXAS LOTTERY® BLACK-SERIES III-LIMITED EDITION
(Austin) ­ The fifth of six $1 million top prizes in the Texas Lottery® Black-Series III-Limited Edition scratch-off game was claimed by Dwayne Shelton of Houston today. The First Stop, 6154 Bellfort St. in Houston, sold the ticket, making the store eligible for a $10,000 retailer bonus. One prize of $1 million and five prizes of $10,000 each remain unclaimed in the Texas Lottery Black-Series III-Limited Edition. Overall odds of winning any prize in the game are one in 3.32, including break-even prizes. A new version of the game, Texas Lottery Black-Series IV-Limited Edition, was introduced yesterday and has nine $1 million top prizes currently unclaimed. Shelton claimed his prize at the Houston Claim Center and requested minimal publicity. Friday's Mega Millions® jackpot is an estimated $25 million. Saturday's Powerball® jackpot is an estimated $44 million, and the Lotto Texas® jackpot is an estimated $11 million. For more information about the Texas Lottery®, please visit www.txlottery.org. ###
