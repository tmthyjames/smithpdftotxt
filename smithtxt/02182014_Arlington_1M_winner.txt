ARLINGTON LOTTERY PLAYER WINS $1 MILLION 50X THE CASH TOP PRIZE

(AUSTIN) ­ Donald M. McKnight of Arlington claimed a $1 million top prize in the 50X The Cash game at the Texas Lottery claim center in Fort Worth. The KB Mart located at 2605 W. Pioneer Parkway in Grand Prairie sold the ticket, making the store eligible for a $10,000 retailer bonus. 50X The Cash is part of the Texas Lottery's new suite of multiplier scratch-off games offered at price points ranging from $1 to $20 which includes 5X The Cash, 10X The Cash, 20X The Cash and 100X The Cash. This suite of games has already paid out a total of four prizes of $1 million each.

With more than $143.7 million in prizes, the overall odds of winning any prize in the game are one in 3.06, including break-even prizes. Eight $1 million top prizes and millions of prizes from $10 to $50,000 currently remain unclaimed.

The claimant requested minimum publicity.

About the Texas Lottery Beginning with the first ticket sold in 1992, the Texas Lottery has generated $21 billion in revenue for the state and distributed $42 billion in prizes to lottery players. Since 1997, the Texas Lottery has contributed $16 billion to the Foundation School Fund, which supports public education in Texas. As authorized by the Texas Legislature, certain Texas Lottery revenues benefit state programs including the Fund for Veterans' Assistance.
