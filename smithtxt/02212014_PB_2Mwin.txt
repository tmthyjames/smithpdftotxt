MULTIPLE MILLIONAIRES CREATED THIS WEEK IN TEXAS!
TEXAS LOTTERY PLAYER WINS $2 MILLION WITH POWERBALL® TICKET PURCHASED IN DEER PARK $1 million scratch-off prizes claimed in Arlington, Bertram and Whitney

(AUSTIN) ­Texas Lottery® games have created multiple millionaires in Texas this week. A Powerball® ticket purchased at the Deer Park Coastal, 4901 W. Pasadena Blvd. in Deer Park matched five numbers (1-1735-49-54) but not the Powerball (34) to win a second-tier prize of $2 million. The prize from the Quick Pick ticket has not yet been claimed. Three other lucky players won $1 million prizes this week.

  

Donald M. McKnight of Arlington claimed a $1 million top prize in 50X The Cash. Jessie Solis of Bertram claimed a $1 million second-tier prize in $500,000,000 Extreme Cash Blast. SFL Revocable Trust, Regina M Perkins, Trustee, of Whitney claimed a $1 million in 20X Ca$h.

About the Texas Lottery Beginning with the first ticket sold in 1992, the Texas Lottery has generated $21 billion in revenue for the state and distributed $42 billion in prizes to lottery players. Since 1997, the Texas Lottery has contributed $16 billion to the Foundation School Fund, which supports public education in Texas. As authorized by the Texas Legislature, certain Texas Lottery revenues benefit state programs including the Fund for Veterans' Assistance.
