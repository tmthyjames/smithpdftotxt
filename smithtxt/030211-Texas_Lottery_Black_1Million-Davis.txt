TEXAS LOTTERY® BLACK SCRATCH-OFF GAME GIVES DALLAS PLAYER TOP PRIZE OF $1 MILLION
(Austin) ­ Alice Davis of Dallas won the top prize of $1 million in Texas Lottery® Black, a scratch-off game the Texas Lottery® released in mid-January. Davis purchased the $10 ticket at the I-35 Texaco located at 6005 SRL Thornton Freeway in Dallas. The store is eligible to receive a bonus of $10,000 for selling this top-prize-winning ticket. Since Texas Lottery® Black was released on January 17, the game has led all sales of scratch-off games in Texas. The game offers six prizes of $1 million, three of which have now been claimed. The game also has 16 second-tier prizes of $10,000, seven of which remain unclaimed, and 104 prizes of $5,000, of which 64 have been redeemed. Davis requested minimal publicity. Tonight's Lotto Texas® jackpot is an estimated $28 million, and the Powerball® jackpot is an estimated $20 million. Friday's Mega Millions® jackpot is an estimated $105 million. More information about the Texas Lottery is on the official website, www.txlottery.org.
