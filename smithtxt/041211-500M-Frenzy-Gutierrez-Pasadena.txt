PRIZE OF $1 MILLION WON BY PASADENA RESIDENT IN $500 MILLION FRENZY SCRATCH-OFF
(Austin) ­Martin Gutierrez of Pasadena won $1 million playing a Texas Lottery® scratch-off game that debuted at the beginning of March, $500 Million Frenzy. "With more than $500 million in prizes, this game took off like gangbusters and tops sales of all our current scratch-offs," said Texas Lottery Commission Executive Director Gary Grief. "The game offers 10 prizes of $2.5 million, all of which are still unclaimed. Mr. Gutierrez won only the second of the game's 40 prizes of $1 million." Gutierrez purchased the winning ticket at Speed, 4203 Fairmont Parkway in Pasadena. The retailer is eligible to receive a bonus of $10,000 for selling this second-tier-prize-winning ticket. The odds of winning any prize in $500 Million Frenzy are one in 2.77, including break-even prizes. Gutierrez requested minimal publicity. Tonight's Mega Millions® jackpot is an estimated $58 million. Tomorrow's Lotto Texas® jackpot is an estimated $13 million, and the Powerball® jackpot is an estimated $27 million. There is more information about the Texas Lottery available on the official website, www.txlottery.org.
