SNYDER RESIDENT CLAIMS $6.75 MILLION LOTTO TEXAS®JACKPOT
(AUSTIN) ­Darrell L. Jones of Snyder visited Texas Lottery® headquarters in Austin on Tuesday to claim the $6.75 million advertised jackpot from the May 11 Lotto Texas® drawing. Jones' Quick Pick ticket was the only ticket sold to match all six numbers drawn, 2 ­ 23 ­ 31 ­ 37 ­ 41 ­ 50. Since Jones chose the cash value option, the amount of his prize was $4,694,564.25, before taxes. The claimant purchased his ticket at the Max-E-Mart #1 located at Interstate Highway 35 and FM 468 (SE corner) in Cotulla. The store is eligible for a $67,500 retailer bonus for selling the winning ticket. The claimant requested minimal publicity. About the Texas Lottery Beginning with the first ticket sold in 1992, the Texas Lottery has generated $21 billion in revenue for the state and distributed $40 billion in prizes to lottery players. Since 1997, the Texas Lottery has contributed $15 billion to the Foundation School Fund, which supports public education in Texas. As authorized by the Texas Legislature, certain Texas Lottery revenues benefit state programs, including the Fund for Veterans' Assistance. The Texas Lottery provides several entertaining games for lottery players including Mega Millions®, Powerball®, Lotto Texas, All or NothingTM, Texas Two Step®, Pick 3TM, Daily 4TM, Cash Five® and scratch-offs.
