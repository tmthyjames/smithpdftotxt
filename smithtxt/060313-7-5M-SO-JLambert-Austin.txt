AUSTIN SCRATCH-OFF PLAYER CLAIMS $7.5 MILLION TOP PRIZE FROM CASINO ACTION GAME
(AUSTIN) ­ Austin resident Jeff Lambert visited Texas Lottery® headquarters on Monday to claim the second of three $7.5 million top prizes in the Casino Action scratch-off game. The $50 scratch-off game features more than $140 million in total prizes ranging from $50 to $10,000, and including one remaining top prize of $7.5 million. Overall odds of winning any Casino Action prize are one in 2.47, including break-even prizes. Lambert bought his winning ticket at the Randalls #2477 located at 3300 Bee Caves Road in West Lake Hills. The retailer is eligible for a $10,000 bonus for selling the winning ticket. The claimant requested minimal publicity. About the Texas Lottery Beginning with the first ticket sold in 1992, the Texas Lottery has generated $21 billion in revenue for the state and distributed $40 billion in prizes to lottery players. Since 1997, the Texas Lottery has contributed $15 billion to the Foundation School Fund, which supports public education in Texas. As authorized by the Texas Legislature, certain Texas Lottery revenues benefit state programs, including the Fund for Veterans' Assistance.
