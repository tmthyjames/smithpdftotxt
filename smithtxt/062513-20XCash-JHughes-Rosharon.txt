$1 MILLION TOP PRIZE WON IN THE 20X CA$H SCRATCH-OFF GAME
(AUSTIN) ­ The Texas Lottery's 20X Ca$h scratch-off game has paid out its second $1 million top prize. James O Hughes purchased the lucky ticket at the Country Food Mart located at 2255 Country Place Parkway in Pearland, making the store eligible for a $10,000 retailer bonus. Introduced in April, the 20X Ca$h features more than $45.8 million in total prizes. In addition to the last $1 million top prize, four second-tier prizes of $10,000 and thousands of prizes ranging from $20 to $1,000 still remain unclaimed. Overall odds of winning any 20X Ca$h prize are one in 3.05, including break-even prizes. The claimant requested minimal publicity. About the Texas Lottery Beginning with the first ticket sold in 1992, the Texas Lottery has generated $21 billion in revenue for the state and distributed $40 billion in prizes to lottery players. Since 1997, the Texas Lottery has contributed $15 billion to the Foundation School Fund, which supports public education in Texas. As authorized by the Texas Legislature, certain Texas Lottery revenues benefit state programs, including the Fund for Veterans' Assistance.
