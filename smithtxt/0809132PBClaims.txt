$1 MILLION POWERBALL® PRIZES FROM SAN ANTONIO AND MOUNT PLEASANT CLAIMED TODAY
Still awaiting word on tickets matching five numbers for Wednesday night's drawing sold in Arlington and Amarillo
(AUSTIN) ­ Two of the four prizes of $1 million or more from Wednesday's Powerball® drawing have been claimed. Peters Eledu of San Antonio and Freddie Walls of Mount Pleasant each claimed $1 million second-tier prizes today. Texas Lottery® officials are still waiting for two other tickets, sold in Arlington and Amarillo, to be claimed. Powerball players win second-tier prizes of $1 million by matching five of the numbers drawn but not the red Powerball. By adding the Power Play® feature for $1 at the time of purchase, players double their second-prize win to $2 million.

About the Texas Lottery Beginning with the first ticket sold in 1992, the Texas Lottery has generated $21 billion in revenue for the state and distributed $40 billion in prizes to lottery players. Since 1997, the Texas Lottery has contributed $15 billion to the Foundation School Fund, which supports public education in Texas. As authorized by the Texas Legislature, certain Texas Lottery revenues benefit state programs, including the Fund for Veterans' Assistance.
