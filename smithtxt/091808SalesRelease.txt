TEXAS LOTTERY® TRANSFERS $983 MILLION TO FOUNDATION SCHOOL FUND IN FISCAL YEAR 2008
(AUSTIN) ­The Texas Lottery® reported that transfers to the Foundation School Fund (FSF) for Fiscal Year (FY) 2008 were $983 million from total sales of $3.67 billion. Both numbers experienced a slight decline in FY 2008 after six consecutive years in which transfers grew and four consecutive years in which transfers exceeded $1 billion. For the year, instant tickets produced sales of $2.78 billion. Lotto Texas® generated sales of $204.6 million, and Cash 5® sales were at $75.8 million, with Texas Two Step® posting sales of $47.9 million. Mega Millions®, the multi-state jackpot game, brought in sales of $174.5 million, and Texas' add-on feature, Megaplier®, added an additional $41.4 million in sales. The final tally for Pick 3TM sales was $286.4 million, while the new addon feature, Sum It UpTM, generated an additional $6.5 million. In September of last year, the Lottery launched Daily 4TM also with the new add-on game feature, Sum It Up. Sales were $42.2 million for the game and $8.3 million for the Sum It Up feature. The Foundation School Fund was not the only beneficiary of the Texas Lottery in the past year. Texas Lottery retailers received more than $183 million in commissions, players won more than $2.27 billion in prizes, and unclaimed prizes, which are transferred to the State General Revenue Fund, totaled $54.2 million.
