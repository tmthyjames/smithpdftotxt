PLAYER FROM GRANBURY WINS $1 MILLION IN NEWEST TEXAS LOTTERY® BLACK GAME
(Austin) ­ In July of this year, the Texas Lottery® introduced the Texas Lottery® Black-Series III-Limited Edition scratchoff game. Less than three months later, Charles T. Dickey of Granbury claimed the second $1 million top prize to be awarded in the game. "The sleek black and gold styling of this ticket may catch your eye," said Texas Lottery Commission Executive Director Gary Grief. "But it's the $1 million top prize that makes the game so exciting." With more than $44 million in total prizes, the Texas Lottery® Black-Series III-Limited Edition game features overall odds of winning of one in 3.32, including break-even prizes. Four top prizes of $1 million each and 11 second-tier prizes of $10,000 remain unclaimed. For selling the winning ticket, the QUIKTRIP #855 located at 6345 Davis Blvd. in North Richland Hills is eligible for a $10,000 retailer bonus. Tonight's Mega Millions® jackpot is an estimated $75 million. Wednesday's Powerball® jackpot is an estimated $25 million, and the Lotto Texas® jackpot is an estimated $7 million.
