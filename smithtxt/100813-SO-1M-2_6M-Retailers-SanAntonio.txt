TWO RETAILERS IN SAN ANTONIO REAP BENEFITS FROM SELLING WINNING TEXAS LOTTERY® SCRATCH-OFF TICKETS
(AUSTIN) ­ Two tickets sold in San Antonio paid out top prizes of $1 million or more today, making two official Texas Lottery® retailers eligible for $10,000 bonuses. William Duncan of La Vista, Nebraska, claimed a 10X Mega Money ticket worth $1 million before taxes from the Travel Centers of America, 6170 Interstate 10 E. Four top prizes currently remain unclaimed in the game, which features overall odds of winning of one in 2.88, including break-even prizes. The other top-prize winning ticket was sold at the Minute Stop located at 4722 Rigsby Ave. San Antonio resident Rosa Linda Sandoval claimed the Bonus Weekly Grand ticket worth $2,500 per week for 20 years, up to $2.6 million before taxes. This was the first of four top prizes in the game, and overall odds of winning are one in 3.96, including break-even prizes. Both claimants requested minimal publicity. About the Texas Lottery Beginning with the first ticket sold in 1992, the Texas Lottery has generated $21 billion in revenue for the state and distributed $41 billion in prizes to lottery players. Since 1997, the Texas Lottery has contributed $15 billion to the Foundation School Fund, which supports public education in Texas. As authorized by the Texas Legislature, certain Texas Lottery revenues benefit state programs including the Fund for Veterans' Assistance. The Texas Lottery provides several entertaining games for lottery players including Powerball®, Mega Millions®, Lotto Texas®, All or NothingTM, Texas Two Step®, Pick 3TM, Daily 4TM, Cash Five® and scratch-offs.
