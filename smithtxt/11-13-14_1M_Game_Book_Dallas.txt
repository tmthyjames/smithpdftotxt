DALLAS RESIDENT CELEBRATES THE HOLIDAYS EARLY WITH $1 MILLION HOLIDAY GAME BOOK PRIZE
(AUSTIN) ­ Brenda S Serpas of Dallas claimed a Holiday Game Book top prize worth $1 million. The ticket was purchased at the Highland Food Store located at 1406 Greenville Ave. in Dallas, making the store eligible for a $10,000 retailer bonus. The claimant requested minimal publicity.

The Holiday Game Book features more than $54 million in total prizes and comes with six different games inside. The overall odds of winning are one in 3.06, including break-even prizes. Serpas claimed the first of four $1 million top prizes in the game.

About the Texas Lottery Beginning with the first ticket sold in 1992, the Texas Lottery has generated $22 billion in revenue for the state and distributed $44 billion in prizes to lottery players. Since 1997, the Texas Lottery has contributed $17 billion to the Foundation School Fund, which supports public education in Texas. As authorized by the Texas Legislature, certain Texas Lottery revenues benefit state programs including the Fund for Veterans' Assistance.
