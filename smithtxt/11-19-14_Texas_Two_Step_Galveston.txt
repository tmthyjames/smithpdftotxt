GALVESTON RESIDENT CLAIMS $1,100,000 TEXAS TWO STEP® JACKPOT
(AUSTIN) ­ Charles W. Ratzman Jr. of Galveston claimed a Texas Two Step jackpot prize of $1,100,000. The winning ticket for the Nov. 13 drawing was purchased at the Kroger located at 5730 Seawall Blvd. in Galveston. The store is eligible for a $10,000 retailer bonus. The claimant requested minimal publicity. Texas Two Step jackpots start at $200,000 and the jackpot amount increases for the next drawing until there is a winner. The game features overall odds of one in 32.4. Texas Two Step drawings are conducted on Mondays and Thursdays at 10:12 p.m. CT. About the Texas Lottery Beginning with the first ticket sold in 1992, the Texas Lottery has generated $22 billion in revenue for the state and distributed $44 billion in prizes to lottery players. Since 1997, the Texas Lottery has contributed $17 billion to the Foundation School Fund, which supports public education in Texas. As authorized by the Texas Legislature, certain Texas Lottery revenues benefit state programs including the Fund for Veterans' Assistance.
