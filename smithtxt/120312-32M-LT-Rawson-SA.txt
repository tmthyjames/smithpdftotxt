$32 MILLION LOTTO TEXAS® JACKPOT PRIZE CLAIMED BY SAN ANTONIO PARTNERSHIP
(AUSTIN) ­ The Rawson SEO Partnership, Richard W. Jones of San Antonio, Trustee, traveled to Texas Lottery® headquarters in Austin today to claim the $24,686,336 cash value of the $32 million advertised jackpot from the Nov. 7 Lotto Texas® drawing. Their Quick Pick ticket was the only ticket sold to match all six numbers, 8 ­ 13 ­ 15 ­ 32 ­ 37 ­ 47. "This partnership has the distinction of claiming the largest Lotto Texas prize awarded in over two years," said Gary Grief, executive director of the Texas Lottery. "But they aren't the only winners for that drawing. More than 21,000 tickets won prizes ranging from $3 to $2,595." The group purchased their ticket at the Thelma Food Store located at 24945 Pleasanton Road in San Antonio. For selling the winning ticket, the store is eligible for a $320,000 retailer bonus. About the Texas Lottery Beginning with the first ticket sold in 1992, the Texas Lottery has generated more than $20 billion in revenue for the state and distributed $39 billion in prizes to lottery players. Since 1997, the Texas Lottery has contributed more than $14 billion to the Foundation School Fund, which supports public education in Texas. As authorized by the Texas Legislature, certain Texas Lottery revenues benefit state programs including the Fund for Veterans' Assistance.
