MEGA MILLIONS® JACKPOT TICKET WORTH $144 MILLION SOLD IN KATY
Could a Powerball® win be next for some lucky Texan?

(Austin) ­ Friday's Mega Millions® jackpot-winning ticket worth an estimated $144 million was sold at the Short Stop Market, 5304 E. 5th St., in Katy. For selling the winning ticket, the store is eligible to receive a retailer bonus of $1 million. With Powerball® tickets going on sale Sunday, January 31, Texans now have the opportunity to play both multi-state jackpot games. Powerball tickets can be purchased at any of the 16,000plus licensed retailer outlets across the state. Could another lucky Texas Lottery ® player win in the Powerball drawing to be held Wednesday, February 3? "We look forward to meeting the person holding the winning Mega Millions ticket," said Texas Lottery Commission Deputy Executive Director Gary Grief. "It would be exciting to have a Texas Lottery retailer sell the winning Powerball ticket on Wednesday night, as well." In celebration of the first Powerball drawing for Texas players, four Customer Appreciation Day events are being held around the state on Wednesday.
