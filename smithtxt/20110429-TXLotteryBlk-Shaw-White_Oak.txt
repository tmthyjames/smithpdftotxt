FOR WHITE OAK RESIDENT, TEXAS LOTTERY BLACK

SERIES II-LIMITED EDITION BRINGS $1 MILLION PRIZE
(Austin) ­ Claude C Shaw of White Oak claimed a Texas Lottery® prize worth $1 million after finding it in a scratch-off ticket, Texas Lottery Black Series II-Limited Edition. "The Texas Lottery Black Series II-Limited Edition ticket continues to be popular among players and remains in the top three in sales of all our scratch-off tickets," said Texas Lottery Commission Executive Director Gary Grief. "Mr. Shaw's prize is the fourth of the game's six top prizes of $1 million in this game to be claimed." Shaw purchased the ticket at the Liberty Food Mart, 5307 FM 1252 West, in Kilgore. The retailer is eligible to receive a bonus of $10,000. Texas Lottery Black Series II-Limited Edition offers more than $44 million in prizes. The odds of winning any prize, including break-even prizes, are one in 3.32. The new winner requested minimal publicity. Tonight's Mega Millions® jackpot is an estimated $40 million. The Lotto Texas® jackpot for tomorrow is an estimated $6 million, and the Powerball® jackpot is an estimated $25 million. More information about the Texas Lottery is available on the official website, www.txlottery.org.
