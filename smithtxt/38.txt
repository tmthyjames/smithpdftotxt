Texas Lottery Commission Press Release - July 11, 2001 Contact: Kristina Tirloni , 512-344-5144

MAYFLOWER, L.P., OF AUSTIN CLAIMS $28 MILLION LOTTO TEXAS JACKPOT
(AUSTIN, July 11) - Today, Mayflower, L.P., of Austin claimed the Lotto Texas jackpot prize for the May 19th drawing, which was worth an estimated $28 million. Mayflower, L.P. is a partnership with Mayflower Enterprises LLC as general partner. Mary Brooker of Chase Manhattan Bank represented the partnership. The partnership chose the Cash Value Option and will receive $15,299,536.00. The Quick Pick ticket was purchased at Park's 66, located at 801 Park Blvd., in Plano. The retailer received a one-percent bonus - $280,000 - for selling the jackpot winning ticket. The partnership has requested minimal publicity. As of today, there are still two jackpot-winning Lotto Texas tickets unclaimed. One was purchased in Humble at Copperfield Liquor #6, located at 7540 FM 1960 East for the $13 million drawing held on Saturday, June 23rd. The other ticket was purchased in Houston at A&L Postal Services, located at 5170 Aldine Mail Rt., Northwood for the $13 million drawing held Saturday, July 7th. Tonight's Lotto Texas drawing is worth an estimated $4 million. .... The Texas Lottery, the 4th largest selling lottery in North America, is "Focusing on the Future of Education, One Ticket At A Time." By providing the 'Games of Texas' for more than 10 years, the Texas Lottery Commission (TLC) has generated more than $10 billion for the state and paid nearly $17 billion to players in prizes.
