TxLottery: News Release

Texas Lottery Commission Press Release - June 15, 2001 Contact: Kristina Tirloni , 512-344-5144

TEXAS TWO STEP - SHOWING PLAYERS THE MONEY!
(Austin, June 15) - - Ask Texas Lottery players and retailers what they like most about the lottery's newest game, Texas Two Step, and you'll hear the number one reason shouted across the state - the money! A Texas Two Step jackpot record is set for Friday, June 15th, when the top-prize for matching all four numbers and the Bonus Ball is $1,100,000. Since the first drawing less than a month ago, Texas Two Step players have won a total of $3,355,623 in prizes. The game has produced 260,477 winners altogether, with the number of winners per drawing averaging more than 30,000. Six jackpot winners have won a combined total of more than $1.1 million. The single largest jackpot won so far has been $350,000 claimed by Rickie Hale of Fort Worth. "We look forward to every Tuesday and Friday night," said Robert Tirloni, Texas Lottery's On-line Product Manager. "It's been very exciting watching for all the new Texas Two Step winners. Since May 18th, we've paid $5 to more than 145,000 winners just for matching the Bonus Ball." The Texas Lottery has paid out more than $240,000 to 151 players who matched the four numbers in the first play field. "The players aren't the only ones winning with Texas Two Step," said Tirloni. "The six retailers who have sold jackpot winning tickets are eligible for more than $11,000 in retailer bonuses combined. Since inception, sales through Wednesday, June 13th, have exceeded $8.8 million." There are two outstanding Texas Two Step tickets still unclaimed. Both tickets were sold in Houston. One was purchased at Fiesta Mart #36, located at 7061 Lawndale for the Friday, May 18th, drawing. The other ticket was sold at Mike's Drive-in Grocery Stop, located at 4702 Ennis Street for the drawing held Friday, June 1st. Texas Two Step is drawn every Tuesday and Friday night at 9:59 p.m. .... The Texas Lottery, the 4th largest selling lottery in North America, is "Focusing on the Future of Education, One Ticket At A Time." By providing the 'Games of Texas' for more than 10 years, the Texas Lottery Commission (TLC) has generated more than $10 billion for the state and paid nearly $17 billion to players in prizes.
