Texas Lottery Commission Press Release - May 31, 2001 Contact: Kristina Tirloni, 512/344-5144

ARLINGTON COUPLE COLLECTS LAST TEXAS MILLION TOP PRIZE
(Austin, May 31) - The Don & Kandy Loggins Revocable Living Trust of Arlington today collected the top prize of $1 million in the Texas Lottery's Texas Million game. The winning numbers were 03-17-22-18. The top-prize winning ticket was purchased at Chuck's Grocery & Grill, located at 700 Secretary Drive in Arlington. The drawing was held on Friday, May 4, 2001. For selling a winning Texas Million ticket, the retailer receives a one-percent bonus of $10,000. There were two tickets sold for the $1 million prize for this drawing. The Partnership of Rojas and Smith of Pharr purchased the other ticket in San Juan at the RC Mini Mart, located at I Road & Chaparral. They claimed their prize on May 25th. The ticket turned over today was the last top-prize winner to be claimed for Texas Million. The last drawing for the game was May 11, 2001. Since its inception in May of 1998, Texas Million produced 25 $1 million winners, and contributed $65 million to the Foundation School Fund. Representatives of the trust requested minimal publicity. .... The Texas Lottery, the 4th largest selling lottery in North America, is "Focusing on the Future of Education, One Ticket At A Time." By providing the 'Games of Texas' for more than 10 years, the Texas Lottery Commission (TLC) has generated more than $10 billion for the state and paid nearly $17 billion to players in prizes.
