Texas Lottery Commission Press Release - February 15, 2001 Contact: Leticia Vasquez, 512/344-5208

CASTROVILLE MAN COLLECTS TEXAS MILLION PRIZE
(Austin, February 15) - Chester A. Suhler, Jr., 40, of Castroville today collected the top prize of $1 million in the Texas Lottery's Texas Million game. Suhler purchased his winning ticket at the Cheers One Stop & More, located at 10317 Sheldon, Suite B, in Houston. The drawing was held on Friday, January 26, 2001. For selling a winning ticket, the retailer receives a one-percent bonus of $10,000. The winning numbers were 29-4-40-60. The numbers were selected randomly. Prizes of $1,000,000 must be claimed at Lottery Headquarters in Austin, and are not official until validated through Lottery Security. Suhler requested minimal publicity. Texas Million drawings are held every Friday at 9:59 p.m. .... The Texas Lottery, the 4th largest selling lottery in North America, is "Focusing on the Future of Education, One Ticket At A Time." By providing the 'Games of Texas' for more than 10 years, the Texas Lottery Commission (TLC) has generated more than $10 billion for the state and paid nearly $17 billion to players in prizes.
