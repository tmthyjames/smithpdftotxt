FAIRFIELD LOTTO TEXAS WINNER COLLECTS $19 MILLION JACKPOT PRIZE
(Austin, January 4) - The Texas Lottery today paid Benson Equities, Ltd., Elvester Benson, president, $760,000, the first installment of a $19 million prize. The Fairfield winner won half of the $38 million Lotto Texas jackpot for the drawing on December 6th, 2000. This is one of the two winning tickets for this drawing. The claimant chose the 25-year annuity option, which will pay out $760,000 every December for the next 24 years. For selling the winning ticket, Rob's Drive In, located at 226 East Commerce in Fairfield, is eligible to receive a one-percent bonus of $190,000. The winning numbers were 35-16-09-01-39-03. The winner requested minimal publicity. The second winning ticket was sold in League City and claimed by CLJ Higgins, Ltd., by ROID Corporation by Chris Cahill in December. Saturday's Lotto Texas jackpot is an estimated $40 million. .... The Texas Lottery, the 4th largest selling lottery in North America, is "Focusing on the Future of Education, One Ticket At A Time." By providing the 'Games of Texas' for more than 10 years, the Texas Lottery Commission (TLC) has generated more than $10 billion for the state and paid nearly $17 billion to players in prizes.
