DEL RIO RESIDENT CLAIMS $1 MILLION TEXAS LOTTERY® PRIZE

(AUSTIN) ­ Rodolfo Rodriguez of Del Rio claimed a $1 million prize from the Texas Lottery scratchoff game, $200,000,000 Cash Blowout. Rodriguez purchased the ticket at the Stripes located at 2100 Veterans Blvd. in Del Rio. For selling the winning ticket, the store is eligible for a $10,000 retailer bonus.

This is the first of the game's top prizes to be claimed. $200,000,000 Cash Blowout features:
· · · · ·

Over $200,000,000 in total prizes 10 top prizes of $1,000,000 10 $100,000 prizes Over 3,770,000 prizes from $20 to $400 Over 2,700 prizes from $1,000 to $10,000

Overall odds of winning any prize in $200,000,000 Cash Blowout are one in 2.82 including breakeven prizes.

The claimant requested minimal publicity. About the Texas Lottery Beginning with the first ticket sold in 1992, the Texas Lottery has generated $21 billion in revenue for the state and distributed $44 billion in prizes to lottery players. Since 1997, the Texas Lottery has contributed $16 billion to the Foundation School Fund, which supports public education in Texas. As authorized by the Texas Legislature, certain Texas Lottery revenues benefit state programs including the Fund for Veterans' Assistance. The Texas Lottery provides several entertaining games for lottery players including Powerball®, Mega Millions®, Lotto Texas®, All or NothingTM, Texas Two Step, Pick 3TM, Daily 4TM, Cash Five® and scratch-offs.
