DUNCANVILLE RESIDENT CLAIMS $1 MILLION TEXAS LOTTERY® SCRATCH-OFF PRIZE

(AUSTIN) ­ James K. Johnston, of Duncanville, claimed a $1 million top prize from the Texas Lottery® scratch-off game, Instant Millions. Johnston purchased the ticket at the Midlothian KWIK Stop located at 4741 N. Highway 67 in Midlothian. For selling the winning ticket, the store is eligible for a $10,000 retailer bonus.

This is the third of five top prizes to be claimed for this game. Instant Millions offers more than $62.3 million in prizes. Overall odds of winning any prize in Instant Millions are one in 2.91 including breakeven prizes.

The claimant requested minimal publicity. About the Texas Lottery Beginning with the first ticket sold in 1992, the Texas Lottery has generated $21 billion in revenue for the state and distributed $44 billion in prizes to lottery players. Since 1997, the Texas Lottery has contributed $16 billion to the Foundation School Fund, which supports public education in Texas. As authorized by the Texas Legislature, certain Texas Lottery revenues benefit state programs including the Fund for Veterans' Assistance.
