Texas Lottery Commission Press Release - February 21, 2003 Contact: Kristina Tirloni, 512-344-5144

Watauga Retailer Receives $40,000 Bonus
(Austin, February 21) - Today the Texas Lottery Commission presented a $40,000 retailer bonus to Albertson's #4193 for selling the jackpot-winning Lotto TexasTM ticket for the drawing held on December 14, 2002. The $4 million jackpot has not yet been claimed. On hand to accept the ceremonial check, Store Director Travis Seitz said, "We are very happy that it may be someone in our community who won the prize." The store, located at 8428 Denton Highway, is the second Albertson's location to sell a Lotto Texas jackpot ticket in Watauga. The ticket is the third jackpot-winning ticket to be sold in the area overall. The Quick Pick ticket was purchased with the Cash Value Option, and is worth more than $2.4 million, before taxes. The numbers drawn were: 40-21-27-03-17-31. "I'm very pleased to present this $40,000 bonus to Albertson's," said Robert Tirloni, Texas Lottery Commission On-Line Product Manager. "Thanks to everyone here today marking this occasion with one of the Texas Lottery's most successful retailers." The two other jackpot-winning tickets sold in Watauga were both for the July 31, 1996, Lotto Texas drawing, worth $16 million. There were three tickets that matched all six numbers for the drawing, and each of the winners received more than $5.6 million. Saturday's Lotto Texas jackpot is worth an estimated $7 million. .... The Texas Lottery, the 4th largest selling lottery in North America, is "Focusing on the Future of Education, One Ticket At A Time." By providing the 'Games of Texas' for more than 10 years, the Texas Lottery Commission (TLC) has generated more than $10 billion for the state and paid nearly $17 billion to players in prizes.
