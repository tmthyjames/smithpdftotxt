Plainview Woman Third Million-Dollar Winner In Texas Lottery Scratch-Off Game

Rhonda Davis Nabors of Plainview said she just had a feeling she would win Holiday Million when she surfed the Texas Lottery website. And she did! Winners Information Specialist Sylvia Womble-Saenz presents Nabors with a ceremonial check for $1,000,000.

(Austin, February 12) - Rhonda Davis Nabors of Plainview said she just had a feeling she would win Holiday Million when she surfed the Texas LotteryTM website (www.txlottery.org) and learned about the new $20 scratch-off game. Today Nabors, her husband and two daughters, visited the Texas Lottery Commission's headquarters in Austin to receive her top prize of $1 million before taxes. "I started looking around on the website one day, and I found the section where upcoming games are featured," explained Nabors. "When I clicked on it, I saw how the ticket looked, what it cost, the odds, and especially, the $1 million top prize. "At that moment, I don't know why, but I just knew I was going to win the $1 million," she said. Nabors bought her winning ticket on January 31 and described what happened next. "I bought a ticket and scratched it in the parking lot, just sitting and staring at it for a while," said Nabors. "I had the clerk run it through the computer, and even though I knew that someday I would win, I started shaking when the clerk said I wasn't imagining." Nabors bought the winning Holiday Million ticket at Allsup's Store #17, 608 South Broadway in Plainview. The retailer is eligible for a bonus of $10,000 for selling the winning ticket. The frequent instant ticket player said she also knew exactly what she wanted to do if she did win: "I want to help our girls go to school, fix up a little around the house and look toward retirement security."
