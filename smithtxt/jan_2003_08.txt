Texas Lottery Offers Players A Chance To Win A 2003 Ford Truck

Bucks 'N Trucks II offers players the chance to win a bright red 2003 Ford F-150 SuperCab XLT pickup truck.

(Austin, January 14) - It's what every Texan has dreamed about at one time or another - owning a big red truck. Now it's possible with Bucks 'N Trucks II, the sequel to one of the Texas Lottery'sTM best selling scratch-off tickets, Bucks 'N Trucks. The new instant game offers Texas Lottery players a ticket to drive into the Texas sunset in a brand new 2003 Ford F-150 SuperCab XLT pickup. "We first offered this game in 1998, and saw it was extremely popular with our Texas Lottery players," said Stephanie Goertz, Instant Product Manager for the Texas Lottery Commission. "Now the Texas Lottery is offering the instant game again and has included all the bells and whistles a winner could possibly imagine on a brand new truck." Bucks 'N Trucks II offers players the chance to win a 2003 Ford F-150 SuperCab XLT pickup truck in bright red. There are a total of 15 Ford trucks to win. Some of the truck's features include: * A V8 "Triton" engine * Automatic 4-speed with overdrive transmission * Four-wheel anti-lock brakes * Power windows and locks * Power steering * Cruise control * AM/FM stereo with CD player * Remote keyless entry * A full tank of gas To play the game, players scratch the ticket and look for a match between the number under "YOUR NUMBERS" and either "LUCKY NUMBER." To win the truck, one of the numbers under "YOUR NUMBERS" must have a truck symbol and match either of the "LUCKY NUMBERS."

The game also gives players a chance to win a top cash prize of $1,000. The cost to play Bucks 'N Trucks II is $2. The odds of winning either a truck prize or any cash prize are 1 in 4.74, including breakeven prizes. Players must claim their truck prize at one of the 22 Texas Lottery Claim Centers throughout the state. Cash prizes under $599 may be redeemed instantly at any Texas Lottery retailer. Cash prizes over $600 may be claimed at any one of the Lottery's Claim Centers or by mail using a claim form. Claim forms are available at the claim centers, in a downloadable format from the Texas Lottery Commission's official website, www.txlottery.org or by visiting a Lottery retailer. .... The Texas Lottery, the 4th largest selling lottery in North America, is "Focusing on the Future of Education, One Ticket At A Time." By providing the 'Games of Texas' for more than 10 years, the Texas Lottery Commission (TLC) has generated more than $10 billion for the state and paid nearly $17 billion to players in prizes.
