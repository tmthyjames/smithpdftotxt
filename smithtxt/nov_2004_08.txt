U.S. Reservist Claims Lotto Texas Prize From July Drawing - Just Home From Tour In Mideast

Lotto Texas winner Brett Parish poses with his ceremonial check.

(Austin, November 10) - Everyone should be so fortunate to have a friend like Dylan. Because of his loyalty to his pal Brett Parish, a United States Reservist in the 345th PSYOPS Company, he's going to get a dinner at the location of his choosing and a TIVO. "I've been stationed in Iraq for almost a year," said Parish. "Dylan was buying my Lotto Texas tickets for me while I was away. In July he emailed that I had won the lottery. It took a few email streams back and forth to get it from him exactly what I had won. I was so excited." Parish collected his $1,427 Lotto prize today at the Texas Lottery Dallas Claim Center. He just returned to the U.S. from a short vacation in Costa Rica. "That's what some of this money is going to pay for, and of course I promised Dylan that dinner." "What a tremendous story of friendship," said Reagan E. Greer, Executive Director of the Texas Lottery Commission. "Congratulations to Mr. Parish for his Lotto win and my sincerest gratitude for his service to this country. "I also want to remind our players of Senate Bill 6 authored by Senator Leticia Van De Putte, enacted in the 2003 Texas Legislative session, which included language for an extension to eligible military personnel to claim a lottery prize if the 180day rule passed before he or she was able to claim a prize."
